Dinner Party Recipe

How to throw a dinner party (through poetry and prose)

Ingredients

use as needed–
improvisation is encouraged.

  • three cups of rhythym
  • one teaspoon of reality
  • 1/2 cup of melancholy
  • 2 1/2 cups of emotional intelligence
  • 1 tablespoon of contentness
  • 2 teaspoons of dramatic endings




Instructions

  1. Open with a soft entrance of descriptive words
    to stimulate the reader's sense. Introduce them
    to your poem's world by trying to embody the different
    elements of what makes it up.

  2. Go into the small details. Paint a more specific image
    by dotting in various touches. Mention the parts that
    catch your eye, whether extravagant or simple.

  3. If there are any present, bring in the people, characters,
    and subjects that exist within your poem's world; give them
    personalities, purposes, and storylines.

  4. This part can encompass any meanings you want.
    It can cover a theme, tell another part of the story,
    or even serve as a simple description.

  5. The ending can go many different ways, dramatically
    or simple or bittersweet. Make sure to touch on your
    theme again in some way and provide some sort of closure
    for your reader to absorb, even if it is one word, sentence,
    or phrase.



The Final Product

honey cakes so soft to the touch
glassy fruit laid out a-front
teacups delicate
and sweet smelling herbs
along with my tea steam
does the air around me stir

white lace dances along the table cloth
and whistling winds chatter with trees
the oven’s excitement
brings about aromas of love
as my window welcomes home
the sun rays from above

my guests range in sizes and smiles
eager to share beautiful flowers of wild
we sit and serve surrounded with petals
and away our sadnesses fly,
to our dreams in which we revel

tart flavors fade into memories of haze
we creatures do love
our afternooning through the day
a celebration of food and flight
and of course dancing to prove off
our superiority of the jive

once again the sunset graces our eyes
her gentle dipping into the horizon
which we so admire
our sadnesses which had lingered
for some mintues too long
follow her home to come back
some other dawn

-anonymous
